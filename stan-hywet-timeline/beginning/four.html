Frank's parents had made a big decision; they were moving to Akron.

“Why do we have to move?” Frank asked, now nearly 6 years old.

“Akron is becoming a happy place to live with lots of factory jobs. It'll be easier for your dad's farm machinery company, J.F. Seiberling & Company,” Catherine said.

Once they moved, Frank went to public school.

He was 15 years old when he enrolled in Heidelberg College. He stayed for two years before joining his dad's company.

On October 12, 1887, he married a woman named Gertrude—Gertrude Penfield, to be exact. They went on to have several children—John, who was named after his grandpa; Irene, who lived until 1999; Willard; James; Gertrude, named after her mother; Grace, who died during infancy; and Frank, who was named after our main character, his dad!

In 1898, he and his brother Charles decided to start a rubber company named Goodyear. He was 38 years old at that time.

By 1916, his company became the largest tire maker in the world.

He used his fortune to solve civic problems; he built a new hospital, created fair housing, improved municipal and national transportation, preserved public green space, and funded many cultural institutions.

“I believe that true prosperity is gained through the improvement of every citizen,” he used to say.

Frank died from pneumonia on August 12, 1955, at the age of 95. His legacy will always be remembered.
